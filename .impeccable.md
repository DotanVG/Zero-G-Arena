## Design Context

### Users
Vibe Game Jam 2026 judges and competitive browser-game players. Playing in a dark room, likely at night, at a desktop. Context is fast, focused, game-session entry — not browsing or reading.

### Brand Personality
**Cold. Precise. Electric.**
Retro-futurist terminal aesthetic — the UI should feel like the HUD of a zero-G combat system. Not "gaming neon slop" but something more tactical and deliberate. Monospace typography is intentional here (not lazy shorthand) because the entire game HUD is monospace — it's the visual language of the game.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark only. Players are in a dark arena at night — a light theme would be jarring.
- **Colors**: Cyan (`#00ffff`) as primary accent (Team 0), Magenta (`#ff00ff`) as secondary accent (Team 1). Neutrals tinted cold-blue, not gray.
- **Typography**: Share Tech Mono — a narrow, technical terminal font with sci-fi character. Not a reflex pick.
- **Background**: The Three.js 3D scene bleeds through the menu at ~7% — intentional. It grounds the UI in the game world.
- **Motion**: Purposeful. Scanline scroll, glow pulse, staggered entrance reveals. Always graceful fallback under `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Decorative**: Minimal HUD-style corner brackets to signal "game interface." Nothing more.

### Design Principles
1. **Contrast must be functional, not decorative** — secondary text can be dim, but never invisible. Minimum 3:1 for tertiary labels.
2. **Every interactive surface needs all states** — default, hover, active, focus-visible. No half-implemented buttons.
3. **Motion is ambient, not intrusive** — animations should recede after the first impression. Players spend seconds on the menu, not minutes.
4. **Monochromatic with surgical color** — the cyan accent is rare and meaningful. Don't dilute it with extra palette entries.
5. **The 3D scene behind is content** — keep background overlays at ≤93% opacity so the arena bleeds through.
