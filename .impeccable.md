## Design Context

### Users
Competitive-leaning gamers and jam participants who play Orbital Breach directly in the browser. Sessions are short (5–15 min), often played solo with bots or in small multiplayer lobbies. Context: desktop browser, dark room, headphones. They care about feel and clarity under pressure, not onboarding.

### Brand Personality
**Terse. Neon. Kinetic.** — like a 1980s arcade cabinet running military targeting software. Every UI element should feel functional and slightly dangerous. Nothing decorative that doesn't serve gameplay.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark (always). Background `#080c14` — near-black with a cold blue cast.
- **Team colors**: Cyan `#00ffff` vs Magenta `#ff00ff` — high contrast, team identity at a glance.
- **Typography**: System monospace only — fits the "hacker terminal in zero-gravity" vibe.
- **Motion**: Purposeful only. Scanlines, glow pulses, typewriter text — never gratuitous bounce.
- **Anti-references**: No rounded corners on cards, no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no hero metrics.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity under pressure** — HUD elements must be readable mid-combat without demanding attention.
2. **Neon economy** — glow effects reserved for live state (active team, active power, live prompts). Inactive = dim.
3. **Instant feedback** — every action (fire, grab, freeze, score) should have immediate visual + audio response.
4. **Zero decoration** — if removing an element doesn't break comprehension, remove it.
5. **Earned polish** — transitions smooth gameplay flow, never interrupt it.
